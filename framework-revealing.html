Framework: Revealing Lies

There are two distinct types of lies and revealing them lets you focus your energy on things that matter.

Lies by omission: Where you leave out important information or fail to correct a pre-existing misconception in order to hide the truth from others.

Lies by deceit: Where you actively present false facts.

Lies by omission are particularly interesting as they are very easy to do. Almost too easy, and they feel like they are doing no damage, maybe even avoiding damage.

However they have strong compounding characteristics i.e. the longer the omission takes place the harder it becomes to reveal and the more damage it does when revealed.

Knowing and understanding these two characteristics of lies of omission provides a strong motivating factor in revealing them as soon as you become aware.

The benefits are again selfish, reveling your lies lets you focus more of your energy on the things that matter.
